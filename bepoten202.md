Pick your Lane

There are only 3 real ways people build wealth: Career, Entrepreneur and Hybrid. Each one works, but each one demands a different type of person.

1. The Career Path: This is actually for people that work a structured role (i.e 9-5 or shift based job). You follow an already built system and prove your skills within a defined role.

As a career worker, you get a predictable income (salary and bonuses). You follow a simple formula:

> Stability
> Growth
> Promotion
> Higher pay

This path suits best the Analyzers, Communicators (cooperate roles, management, HR) and some builders (engineers, dev).

Sometimes it takes long term to see actual real wealth results;

— Short-term: 3–6 months (steady income)
— Mid-term: 2–5 years (career growth, promotions)
— Long-term: 10+ years (real wealth if invested properly)

But the hidden risks nobody tells you about this path is that your income is capped by someone else, your job security is not guaranteed, you can get comfortable and stop growing and your time is traded directly for money

2. The Entrepreneur Path: Here you build something from nothing, with guaranteed income, with constant problem-solving and you wear multiple hats (marketing, sales, product, etc.)

Your life is moving from: Chaos → Learning → Failure → Breakthrough

This best suits builders that love creating, performers that are great at attention & brand and connectors who are perfect at partnerships & growth

Sometimes it might take longer to build into wealth;
— Short-term: 0–12 months (usually struggle phase)
— Mid-term: 2–5 years (traction)
— Long-term: 5–10+ years (real wealth)

Yet, there are also some hidden risks nobody tells you; 

. You can work hard for years with no payoff
. Income is unpredictable
. Burnout is very real
. Most businesses fail quietly

3. The Hybrid Path (The Smart Play Right Now): This is where things get interesting especially for young Africans. In this path, you’re combining both a stable income source (job, freelance, or gigs) with scalable assets (contents, skills, digital leverage).

A typical hybrid life be like;

— Morning: client work / job
— Afternoon: skill-building
— Night: content / personal brand / side income

It’s as simple as moving from: Income → Skill → Audience → Leverage → Freedom

This path best suits the Communicators (content, writing, storytelling), Builders (freelancing, design, dev), Connectors (community, growth, deals)

Hard Truth is that Hybrid rewards multi-skilled people and it probably takes less time to build wealth compared to the others;

— Short-term: 1–3 months (first income online)
— Mid-term: 6–18 months (consistent income)
— Long-term: 2–5 years (real independence)

Somehow, there are also some hidden risks nobody tells you;

— It’s basically 2 lives at once (can be exhausting)
— Easy to get distracted and chase everything
— Requires strong discipline (no boss forcing you)
— Slow at first → many people quit too early

For example, MrBeast started by making videos with no money while reinvesting everything. Now he runs a media empire plus businesses.

The years of posting with zero/limited results pays off eventually.

For the sake of this book, I will be more focused on the Hybrid Path which is the Most Relevant for You.

Living Beyond Your Potential

There is a proven fact that most people gets tired in their first 3 months of trying out new things. In this chapter, I will expose you to possible reasons and how to overcome them.

A pattern almost nobody talks about:

— First 2-3 weeks of starting is the period of motivation. Out of excitement, you post daily, learn fast and even everything feels possible.

— Next 4-6 weeks is when reality start setting in and in this phase, results are slower than expected. Growth is inconsistent and things are not even going like you envisioned it yet.

— Week 7-9 is when you feel completely silence since there are no big wins, no validation, nothing seen to be working.

This is when most people quit and give up. And trust me I have been there before. It was almost at this point I gave up on my first business when I was in 200 level after investing over a million into it.

Not because I was incapable but because I expected faster results than reality allows.

To avoid this, you need to expect this phase even before it comes. Instead of thinking - “If this doesn’t work in 3 months, I’ll stop…”, Think - “The first 3 months don’t count. I’m just building momentum.”

To be very honest, that might be all you need to keep you in the game longer than 90% of people.

This will bring me to differentiate between Motivation and Discipline.

Right after you started reading this book, you must have been motivated and eager to start. But trust me, motivation is not reliable because it’s a feeling; it comes and goes and depends on mood, energy, environment. If you rely on motivation, you will be inconsistent.

But think of Discipline as a system. As in showing up even when you don’t feel like it, having a routine you follow automatically, removing decisions so you don’t rely on willpower

A clear example is you saying you will work when you feel inspired and you will work everyday at 7pm without debate. One speaks for motivation and the other for discipline.

Build a system and abide by it such that you can’t avoid it: 

— 1 hour daily fixing your brand
— 1 post per day on social media
— Networking someone important daily

This small but repeatable non-negotiable acts are what builds your discipline.


Time to know and understand how to Set Targets that stretch you without breaking you. Pay attention to this part.

Most people fail here, because they aim too high and burn out or aim too low and stay stagnant.

Your targets should feel slightly uncomfortable but still doable. So let’s admit a 70 percent rule fo the sake of this book.

If you can complete your goal 70–80% of the time, it’s perfect. For examples: post 5 times a week (instead 7), work 2 focused hours daily (instead of 10), reach out to 10 clients (instead of 100).

Looks behind but doing something and it’s actionable and doable. Not bothering you but easy to stay consistent with. Because consistency beats intensity, you don’t need to go hard for 2 weeks; you need to stay consistent for 2 years.

Most people are addicted to big wins, but real success comes from: small actions repeated over time — the compound effect.

People ignore this because the results are invisible at first and there is no applause or instant reward.

Meanwhile in reality, 1 post a day amounts to 365 posts a year, 1 networking daily amounts to huge net worth in 12 months and 1 client/month gives full income in a year.

Individually, these actions look small, but when stacked over time, they change your life. Everything starts compounding:

— Your skills sharpen
— Your network grows
— Opportunities increase

Then, it looks like overnight success. Meanwhile, it’s only a delayed result.
